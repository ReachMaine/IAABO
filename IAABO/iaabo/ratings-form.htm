Ratings Form Clarifications

Availability

  • Two points received for 0 - 7 blocked dates
  • One point received for 8 - 15 blocked dates
  • Zero points received for more than 15 blocked dates
    (The Assigner may deduct one or both availability points for lack of cooperation)

Attendance

  • Members receive 1 point each for attending the Fall & Spring Business Meeting.
  • The Annual Fall Conference will be a one day event worth credit for 3 or 4 clinics. Credit for one or two clinics will also be given for attending regional review session(s) other than the Refresher Test Review.
  • The December and January clinics will count as one clinic. (Total of 7) Once a member has attended the 5 clinics for their 15 points, then additional clinics would be worth 1/2 point bonus credit for each.
  • There will not be clinic points offered for attending the Prospective Officials Class, these would only count towards a member being in good standing
    (Requirement of attending three clinics)

Evaluation

  • Fellow Officials Ratings will be worth 15 points. Five points will be deducted from this total for not submitting the Fellow Officials Rating Form. When tabulating the Fellow Officials Ratings a high and low rating will be thrown out. An Official must receive 10 Fellow Officials Ratings to be eligible to receive these points.
  • A School Rating will also be worth 15 points. An official must receive 5 School Ratings in order to be eligible to receive these points.
    (Both Fellow Officials and School Ratings will be multiplied by 1.5 to accumulate the 15 point value in each category. If an official does not receive enough ratings in one category then the other category will be multiplied by 3)
  • Evaluators / Observers comments will be used for self improvement plus shared with the Assigner only and will not be involved in the ratings.

Physical / Eye Exam

  • Physical / eye exams will be required every other year and worth 3 points.

Sportsmanship Ballot

  • A sportsmanship ballot will be requested of each person officiating varsity games. It will be asked to select your top three choices of boys and girls varsity teams in rank order. All those turning in this form will receive one point.
    (Those not working varsity games can receive their point by turning in a blank form stating “No varsity games worked”)

Experience Points

  • One point for completing 75 high school level games (Frosh, JV, Varsity)
  • Two points for completing 50 varsity games
  • Three points for completing 100 varsity games.
    (An individual could receive one experience point for making the tournament officiating pool within the past three years but their experience bonus points could never be more than three points)

Levels

  • Level One = Officials with a rating of 87.500 to 98 (Primarily all varsity assignments)
  • Level Two = Officials with a rating of 83 - 87.499 (Mixture of varsity & sub-varsity assignments)
  • Level Three = Officials with a rating of 78.500 - 82.999 (Primarily sub-varsity but could work a varsity assignment with a Level One or Level Two official)
  • Level Four = Officials with a rating of 72 - 78.499 (Mainly sub-varsity schedule. If needed in emergency for a varsity game, would have to work with a Level One official.)
  • Level Five = Officials rated 71.999 and below would work primarily middle school games or a few sub-varsity high school games. No varsity games.
    (Anyone not passing the written test for three consecutive years would automatically be placed in Level Five)
