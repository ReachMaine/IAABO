Clinic Notes from January 4, 2009

Uniforms / Equipment

  • Continue to enforce, direct to leave game if not complying with shirts being tucked in. (Wait until all duties have been completed on next dead ball and once ball is ready to be put in play if player has not tucked in their shirt then ask for a substitute)
  • Undergarments, which are compression shorts or tights, includes Under Armour type wear, must be predominant color of pant / skirt and cannot be below knee or below pant / skirt. If players insist to roll pants over at waist make sure manufacturer’s label does not show.
  • Compression sleeves must be for medical reasons

Rough Play

  • Don’t allow displacement, bumping cutters, backing down, swim strokes or hooking
  • Piling on for a loose ball should be ruled a foul, not a jump ball. Also, in trying to secure a loose ball there may be incidental contact but don’t ignore illegal contact that puts one player at a disadvantage of getting to the loose ball.

Time-outs

  • Make sure to identify the source and the request then check to see if there is player control. Be sure to indicate the throw-in spot immediately after recognizing the time-out. Remember an Interval of Time is not to be treated the same as a time-out.

Slapping the Backboard

  • We have been penalizing due to technical fouls turned in, good job ! It can be a legal act but don’t allow as an act of frustration or bringing attention to oneself.

Closely Guarded

  • Know what 6’ is, (distance from top of arc to foul line), may be changing counting arms continuously in some situations. Count ends when dribbler has head & shoulders by defender. Just because a player is moving towards the basket does not terminate the count if they have not beaten the defender.

Free throw lane

  • Remember, feet cannot break the plane. Eliminate players having arms out in front of opponent before putting ball at disposal of free thrower. Don’t allow players to step in behind opponent before ball hits the rim.

Traveling

  • There is always need for improvement with this ruling. If it is so close that you need a video replay to determine if there was a travel then it probably is a good indication that we should pass on a call. Remember, a jump stop must be simultaneous landing on both feet with no ability to pivot. Identify the pivot foot, don’t allow step backs or throughs that move the pivot foot.

Continuous motion

  • Must be a foul by the defender to apply. Foul may not be on the shooter but continuous motion would still apply.

Interrupted Dribble

  • If dribbler steps on boundary line during interrupted dribble it is not a violation as it is if player is in control, dribbling. ( Does not have to be touching ball when dribbling and stepping on line)
  • Wrong # in book is a technical foul but not charged to the head coach as an indirect as is an illegal # or uniform
  • When notifying the coach of loss of coaching box after a direct or indirect technical foul, even though the Manual indicates it is the Referee’s responsibility, it may be best to have the non-calling official handle this duty so as not to aggravate the situation anymore.
  • Intentional fouls must be called if there is no attempt to play the ball. Time remaining in game has no bearing, if player is not playing the ball then an intentional foul is suspect.
  • Don’t initiate talk with coaches. Answer questions then move on. Stay away from coaches during touchy situations, don’t agitate the problem anymore.
  • Be professional in all that you do, from your dress and appearance, to early arrival at game site, to greetings and talking to coaches prior to changing up. We are the integrity of the game, don’t diminish that ! Always work hard no matter what the situation and don’t ever feel that our duties are easy and taken for granted. We are always learning and can improve.

Two Person Mechanics Needing Attention

  • During pre-game conference be sure to touch on these touchy areas;
    1. Far sideline above free throw line extended.
    2. End line A, B, C positions and closing down.
    3. Presses and spread offense
    4. Out of bounds near division line in transition
    5. PCA’s and calling out of PCA
  • Use the bounce pass to improve angles and field of vision.
  • As Trail on free throws, don’t be too deep. 2/3 of way to sideline and one step behind free throw line extended. Know people responsible for watching and don’t leave until ball hits rim then step towards end line. Be aware of those outside the arc. Lead stay out of visual field of shooter. May help to see people responsible for by being up to boundary line instead of off the end line.

Three Person Comments

  • Be sure to close down and ready to rotate when ball is on other side of basket line
  • If Center has pressure deep on their side in front court then the Lead should rotate to release the Center to the Trail position.
  • Lead be sure to watch play over shoulder in transition and help with side line boundary calls.
  • Center take a major role in cross court calls in transition.
  • Lead be sure to stay wide when ball is on your side of basket line.
  • Trail will have responsibility for most back court violation calls.
