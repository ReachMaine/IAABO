Mid-Season Notes 2008

RULES:

1) Impress upon officials that each and every situation and the resulting ruling (legal or illegal) matters to both teams. Simple as it may seem, officials must carry that thought throughout the game.

Examples:

  • late in game potential intentional foul situations crashes, one player is knocked down and now can’t defend or rebound

  • player on floor and a pile on that results in a jump ball

  • delaying for a substitute during free throw or throw-in, one team is may be ready

  • delaying at the end of a time-out, one team may be ready.

2) Traveling –

  • identify the pivot foot

  • to pass or try for goal – pivot foot maybe lifted, just can’t retouch

  • need to know when the ball was gathered/controlled and then when the foot became the pivot foot

  • too many legal move penalized = when so, a problem

  • too many illegal moves not penalized = when so, a problem

3) Carrying/Palming

  • care about it, watch the player dribble-concentrate

  • it is not simply about the terms carrying/palming, it is about a dribble ending (ball coming to rest/suspended, ends the dribble)

  • to allow the ball to come to rest/become suspended (ends the dribble), then to allow a dribble is to allow an illegal dribble (double dribble)

  • to not penalize, can lead to a great advantage for the dribbler and a great disadvantage for the defender

4) Continuous Motion (factors to be aware of):

  • the foul must be committed by the defense

  • the try requirement is that it need only to have merely begun

  • the foul, the sounding of the whistle and the signal to stop the clock, only stops the clock

  • the ball remains live and the try may be completed

  • the position of a foot or the feet, in reference to the floor, are not factors within the rule involved

  • the “shooter” does not have to be in the final stage of the “act of shooting”

  • what has the “shooter” done wrong?

  • the “shooter” is simply carrying out his/her basketball given right of attempting a try for goal – officials need to be sure to not end that right. If the try has merely begun, it must be allowed to continue.

5) Closely Guarded – the rule applies during drives toward the basket

6) Guarding – Block/ Charge – situations near the basket/under the basket are to be ruled just as such situations are any where else on the court. What has the defender done wrong? He/she is playing as per rule.

7) Rules regarding proper wearing of uniforms must be enforced. Warnings are not enforcing the rule. This has been a POE for several years. When an official does not comply with enforcement, it encourages non-compliance. This is another case where officials must realize there are two teams. One team is complying, the other team is not complying and the official is warning. Fair play?

8) Reminder, at all levels of basketball – enforcement of rule is “in vogue”

MECHANICS:

  1. Have a pre-game conference. Be in the dressing room shortly after the 3rd quarter begins. To do otherwise, does not allow ample time to stretch, dress, groom and have a professional conference.
     

  2. Pre-game on the court -- use the time wisely – identify pivot foot - jump shots, post player moves, 3 point attempts, etc.
     

  3. Continue to work at mastering PCAs and responsibilities – develop a mental impression of each PCA.
     

  4.  —Learn to accept the ball when it enters your PCA
     —Learn to give up the ball when it enters a partner’s PCA
     —Practice the concept of Field of Vision Cones – learn to adjust them
     —Check your individual coverage responsibilities – when the situation permits, expand your FOV cone and thereby improve the crew’s coverage
     

  5. To start each quarter or extra period, sound the whistle, indicate the direction of play, announce the color
    After each time-out or unusual delay, sound the whistle, indicate the direction of Play, announce the color.
     

  6. At the site of every foul, complete all of the information and signals – be sure to inform a partner of the number of free throws and the number of the free thrower.
     

  7. During time-outs, be alert – dead ball time matters—problems can arise – officials seem to be into a habit of gathering during time-outs. Only do so when absolutely necessary.
     

  8. After granting a time-out with the whistle and time-out signal, immediately indicate the throw-in spot.
     

  9. Don’t take the rule responsibility of approving the final score lightly. There is no need to race off of the court – what are we running from? Did we perform that poorly? What kind of an impression does that leave upon someone who notices that practice. Yes, there are exception situations – they are very rare.
     

  10. During time-outs, after the first horn inform properly then go directly to your position. Be ready to put the ball in play as per rule, don’t hurry doing so, don’t delay doing so. There are two teams involved.
     

  11. Substitutions, do not delay the game to allow for substitutes who are not at the table and ready. Coaches hollering or substitutes running to the table are not indications that a substitute is ready. There are two teams involved.
     

  12. During free throws, the Lead official’s position is not within a lane line space.
     

  13. When communicating with the scorer regarding the number of fouls, ask for the “number of team fouls” and not “are we in the bonus?”
     

  14. At the site of a team control foul all other “at the site of foul” procedures are to be completed.
     

  15. Don’t signal "not closely guarded" when offensive and defensive player’s feet are within six feet of one another.
     

  16. Non-signals and unorthodox signals may cause confusion. Use only approved signals.

SIGNALS:

1) Stop the clock with the appropriate signal upon each whistle (foul, violation, jump ball/held ball, time-out.

2) Designate each and every throw-in spot on such throw-ins.

3) Count every backcourt situation.

4) Take your time – don’t hurry arm up and down, etc.

5) After a violation ruling, stay there and complete all signaling – don’t turn your back and start moving away while signaling.

6) The Lead official does not signal the number of free throw(s) after the initial indication within the free throw lane.

7) When ruling a foul that will result in 2 free throws for a player fouled while attempting a try for goal (including continuous motion), the ruling official should not immediately signal “two” with his/her fingers raised above their head. Do not signal two free throws until the appropriate time and it is clear that there is no goal tending or basket interference.
