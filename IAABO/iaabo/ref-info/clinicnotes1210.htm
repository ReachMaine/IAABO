Clinic notes from
December 12 2010

  • Regarding symptoms of concussion and player replacement, let the Referee deal with this situation since it is an administrative type duty.

  • Don’t be a ball watcher, master your PCA, trust your partner, widen your vision cone when the ball is out of your area and call what needs to be called out of your PCA, not what you think is a violation or foul.

  • Enforce the rules as is the Point of Emphasis this year. If someone misbehaves penalize it with a technical foul. Don’t tolerate it then talk about it later or tell the coach the next time you see them that they better behave this game. Don’t carry grudges from one game to another either. What happened in the previous game is over, start anew and deal with issues as they arise.

  • Penalize blatant flopping with a technical foul. It most likely will stop such actions, just like removing players with uniforms untucked.

  • Error on the side of continuous motion as we do with player control. If it is close consider the player has started the motion.

  • Ignore buzzers when the ball is live.

  • Proper terminology on throw-in violations would be moving the endline not traveling the endline. There are not foot/pivot limitations out of bounds.

  • It is not the non-calling officials responsibility to know the shooter on a foul involving shots. The non-calling official may be aware of the shooter once the calling official identifies such but the non-calling should not be doing the initial identifying.

  • There is not a signal for a flagrant foul. The procedure is call the foul and then state that it is either a personal or technical flagrant foul which results in ejection from the game.

  • Players who happen to foul out during the first half of a game may still warm up with their team at half-time. If a player is ejected though in the first half, they may not be allowed to practice with their team at half-time.

  • If a thrower-in never gets out of bounds at all to make the throw-in it is a throw-in violation, not a do over.

  • Only 2 players are allowed out of bounds at the same time for a non-designated throw-in. (Refs School has some incorrect statements on this play)

  • Remember, an Interval of Time for replacing a disqualified, injured or bleeding player does not allow anyone other than the Head Coach, 4 players and substitute to be standing. Be conscious of this and address the issue when not being followed by reminding the bench personnel that they must be seated during an Interval of Time.

  • When you have an injured player and you have beckoned the coach onto the floor, even if the coach refuses to come, once you have beckoned, then the player must be replaced or the Coach must take a time-out to keep the player in the game. Once you have notified the Coach that a replacement is needed then they must request a time-out at this time to keep the player in the game, otherwise once you start the Interval of Time it is too late to request the time-out. They can ask for a time-out after the player is replaced but the player replaced cannot return until after the clock has started.

  • A player breaking down the rim with their forearm should be considered the same as a player grasping the rim, penalized with a technical foul.

  • As per a Casebook play from last year an Intentional Foul committed on a player in the act of shooting a three point try, the penalty is three shots, not the typical two as is customary with most Intentional Fouls.
