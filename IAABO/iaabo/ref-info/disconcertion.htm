DISCONCERTION IS A CONCERN!

Officials should not take "Disconcertion" (actions by opponents of the free-thrower, physical and verbal actions) lightly.

As per rule 4.20.1, when a foul is ruled on a player or team and the free-throw (s) is/are awarded, the free-thrower is guaranteed an UNHINDERED opportunity to score.

Officials, rather than observe actions by the opponent (s) and rule that the actions are "not to bad", ask yourself "Why is the opponent (s) doing it"?

Further, these actions take place even when the ball is not going to be playable (it becomes dead) when the try ends. This is a situation where the official gives the player(s) in the wrong, the benefit, and the player who has a rule, given the special right, does not receive a benefit.

The rules are in place, why not enforce them as they intend the official to do ? If the rules were properly enforce, how much "Disconcertion" would take place? None, the issue would be eliminated!

References: Rules 4.20.1,9.1.5 Penalty 2(a+b), casebook 9.1.5A
