MID-SEASON MEMO

TO: IAABO Interpreters
FR: Peter Webb, Coordinator of Interpreters

Professionalism, maintain a high level

  • Care about appearance, proper attire to game; uniform sharp

  • Arrive early; be in dressing room early

  • Eliminate gossip; negative talk about fellow officials, coaches  players and the Board/Association; such conduct is inappropriate, unethical and does not help anyone or anything

  • Don’t fraternize with coaches; address coaches as “coach” not by first name

  • Treat every game with respect; be accountable

  • Honor the high school game and its expectations

  • Care about everything because everything matters

Pre- Game Conference

  • A detailed thorough pre-game conference is a must

  • It is preparation time, it forces the crew to focus on the officiating task at hand

Foul Call, the priority is to identify the free thrower to a partner

  • Be sure the “freeze” coverage is applied as non-ruling official

  • Take your time (if it helps to slow you down, use the bird dog signal), remain at the site, present the required at the site info prior to leaving to report

  • Check the manual; get to the Reporting Area, not almost

  • Hustle, when moving to and from the Reporting Area

  • Don’t “walk & talk” while leaving the site of the foul or while getting to the Reporting Area

Talking to coaches or players
 Don’t delay the game to talk; don’t talk during free throw coverage time; don’t initiate conversation; be a listener when time permits; have compassion for players and coaches when possible yet always take care of business; in very rare exceptions can one improve upon silence.

Warnings
There are NO warnings, other than the four allowed by rule; enforce the rules.

Free throw Coverage
Observations find that officials are very inattentive to free coverage responsibilities 

  • Check the manual for proper positioning

  • Care about disconcertion ..Enforce the rules

  • Care about free throw lane line violations…Enforce the rules

Primary Coverage Area (PAC)

  • Check the manual to familiarize yourself

  •  Learn to accept the ball as it enters into your PCA (my ball)

  •  Learn to give up the ball when it leaves your PCA (her/his  ball)

  •  Learn to not “ball watch” prior to the ball entering your PCA

  •  Learn to trust your partner(s)

  •  Learn to be careful/patient about ruling across the basketline

  •  As Lead, don’t turn your head toward the sideline – move to wider positioning

  •  As T or C in Crew of 2 or 3, prior to transition – remain and read the situation’s coverage needs

  • Lead, when implementing a rotation (going from B to C) don’t run/dash to the C position; while hustling to the C position keep your head turned to officiate your PCA coverage  responsibilities; too often officials are not officiating while moving, the objective seems to be to simply get to the other side of the basketline.

  • Trail, C of 2 or C of  3, get in the game, 3-point arc as a guide

  • Keep in mind the “Field of Vision Cone” concept and constantly be aware of moving to improve your coverage angle.

Jump Ball

  • Care about the toss; request a re-toss; it matters

  • During loose ball, don’t turn a player piling on to an opponent into a jump ball ruling. It is a foul!

  • During trapping situations, don’t allow bumping, leaning, hitting arms, etc. to end up resulting in a jump ball ruling; It is a foul!

  • A blocked “shot” isn’t always a jump ball ruling

  • Alternate possession rulings, look to table facing partner for direction of arrow rather than turning, peaking to see the arrow

Granting Time Out

  • While ball is live and in play, player control must exist – be careful; a ball pinned to the floor is not control

  • Upon granting a time out, after sounding the whistle and signaling, immediately indicate/identify the throw-in spot

  • Be patient prior to reporting and starting the time out clock

  • DO NOT allow an Interval of Time to replace a player who has been directed to the bench to be treated (by official or coach) as a time-out

Technical Foul

  • Don’t personalize it; it is simply a rule that requires a penalty

  • Take your time; don’t over react; always sound the whistle and      stop the clock with a foul signal first; signal the technical foul; proceed to the Reporting Area; if it is charged to the Head Coach or bench personnel, inform the coach of the loss of the coaching box

  • Because a coach has been penalized with a technical foul does not mean that a rebuttal time is allowed the coach

  • Confer with your partner(s) then proceed with the administration of  the penalty 

  • DO NOT DISCUSS A TECHNICAL FOUL OR AN EJECTION OF A PLAYER OR COACH beyond the confines of the gymnasium. Doing so is very unethical. The penalty is enough. What happened in that gymnasium stays in that gymnasium.

Closely guarded
Apply the rule immediately, it matters; realize what six feet is; the defense is not required to be intensely guarding an opponent; there is no signal for not closely guarded
Substitution, don’t delay the game to admit a substitute; allow the score and timer to do carry out their duties; there must be a horn

Enforce - palming/carrying
We are doing a good job, keep it up.

Enforce – hand checking/hands on, bumping, “measuring the opponent”
These are not legal contact (we are not doing as well as we should and are being criticized) coaches are expecting this contact to be penalized! It is a Point of Emphasis!

Enforce contact during trapping situations and on dribblers in the backcourt.
Players just don’t lose the ball, way more often than not video replay easily shows that contact caused the loose ball

Enforce - displacement of shooters and rebounders when players are “boxing out”
 “Boxing out” does not require contact nor “putting a body on them”.

Enforce – the coaching box rule
It is rather simple and very specific; there will only be one penalty, coaches will comply and then the game will be better for all involved; box boundaries are to be treated like other boundary lines (the coach is out of bounds); “it doesn’t bother me if a coach is out of the box as long as he is coaching” is the response of some officials…neither should it bother an official if a player steps out of bounds…in both cases, enforce the rules.

Kicking must be intentional
 A ball coming in contact with a moving foot is not kicking; a moving foot that crosses the path of a ball is not kicking; a defender has a right to move her/his foot/feet.

A concern that is surfacing
A block/charge situation must not be turned into a traveling ruling when there is no traveling violation. Video does not lie and there is a lot of video around.

Stop the clock on every whistle by displaying the proper stop the clock signal.

Always demonstrate composure…getting excited/over emphatic  when ruling and signaling (“selling the call”) may leave the impression that you are emotionally involved in the game; it may indicate that a block, charge, time-out request, last second traveling violation, etc. are of more importance/more special than other situations and rulings; getting the involved ruling right is the best form of “selling”; sound the whistle, take your time, signal sharp/crisply while being poised like a professional who has made such rulings thousands of times before.

This year much emphasis has been placed on the enforcement rules of the game. Whether it involves contact, carry/palming, free throw violations, control/time-outs, coaching box or sportsmanship……..SIMPLY TRUST IN THE RULES AND ENFORCE THE RULES…THE PLAYERS AND COACHES WILL ADJUST. Players want to play and coaches want to coach. The game will be better for all involved.

Best wishes for an enjoyable and successful remainder of the officiating season.
