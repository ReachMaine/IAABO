Clinic notes from 12/13/09

    Overall the Board did very well on the written rules test. 31 members got all 50 questions correct with 72 members missing 2 or less and 84 missed 3 or less.  The average # of questions missed was 2.491.  The two most missed questions were #7 and #16.  The Mechanics test results were not quite as good which substantiates what Commissioner Webb has been telling us that we need to spend more time in the Manual like we do in the Rule Book. Ten members got all 30 questions right on the Manual test with the average     # missed being 3.991.  The most missed questions on the Manual exam were #’s 2, 9, 14,  & 22.  Six members got 100% on both tests.

    General comments;

       1.  Veterans be sure to set a good example with mechanics and signals.  Newer officials are observing, therefore we need to be handling our duties responsibly and properly so that they do not pick up bad habits.

       2.  Newer officials make sure to learn the coverage responsibilities regarding your PCA as a Trail and Lead.  Master them as you are officiating. Do not be a ball watcher !  If the ball is out of your PCA, make sure you are covering all those players in your PCA that do not have the ball but who may be pushing, setting illegal screens or committing violations.

       3.   Remember, we are not to refuse to shake hands with players prior to the game.  It is not the proper message to be sending teams.

       4.   If a player needs to be removed due to a shirt being untucked during a dead ball when two or more free throws are to be taken, then any substitutes properly reporting to the table may also come into the game with the substitute for the replaced player. This is considered the same procedure as when an injured or disqualified player is being replaced.

    Specific Play Situations

       1.  If a player, who is making a throw-in, loses their balance and falls forward but prevents stepping over the line by touching the ball to the floor inbounds, this player has committed a throw-in violation.

       2.  A jump stop is when a player, with the ball, lands on one foot, jumps off that foot and then lands simultaneously on both feet. This player cannot pivot or step, they can only lift a foot to pass or shoot.  This is different than a two foot landing, which is when a player, who is in the air, lands with both feet simultaneously.  This player may now step with either foot, then establishing the other foot as the pivot foot.  Try not to mix these two situations up.

       3.  Casebook play 4.19.5, this is an intentional technical foul which has the same penalty as any technical foul, two shots and the ball at the division line opposite the table.

       4.  Casebook plays 4.19.8C & 4.19.9A should be reviewed to understand  penalties and rulings regarding double or false double fouls involving an airborne shooter.

       5.  Casebook play 5.8.3E clarifies the difference between mistakenly granting a time-out and an accidental whistle involving what you thought was a time-out request.

       6.  Casebook play 4.34.1 & 2 clarifies bench personnel during time-outs or between periods or intermissions.

       The last half hour of the clinic was used watching video clips of play situations provided by IAABO  with “You Making the Call “!
